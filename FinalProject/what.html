What is Universal Design?

Universal Design (UD) is an inclusive design philosophy that aims to create environments, products, and services that are accessible to as many people as possible, regardless of age, disability, or other factors. This approach not only considers the accessibility for individuals with disabilities but also the broad spectrum of human abilities and preferences. The goal is to produce solutions that are inherently usable by everyonewithout the need for adaptation or specialized design.

Advanced reasoning in UD goes beyond mere compliance with accessibility standards; it involves a proactive consideration of diverse user needs throughout the design process. This can include ergonomic design for physical comfort, clear and intuitive user interfaces for cognitive ease, and aesthetic considerations that make products and environments not only functional but also pleasurable to use. By embracing these principles, designers and developers can create more inclusive, equitable, and sustainable solutions that benefit a wider range of users.

Norman's Design Concepts

Concept Description Example
Affordance Affordances are the perceived and actual properties of an object that suggest how it can be used. The shape of a computer mouse suggests its use: it fits comfortably in the hand and buttons are positioned where fingers naturally rest.
Constraints Constraints are limitations or restrictions intentionally imposed on a design to guide users and prevent unintended actions. Software installation wizards that grey out the "Next" button until all necessary fields are filled out correctly.
Feedback Feedback is information provided to users about the results of their actions. A light on a laptop power button that turns on when the device is powered up, indicating that it is operational.
Visibility Visibility refers to the clarity and prominence of an object or interface element. A brightly colored "Submit" button on a web form, clearly indicating where to click to send the information.
Mapping Mapping involves the relationship between controls and their effects. The volume knob on a speaker system that turns clockwise to increase volume and counterclockwise to decrease it, mimicking the natural motion of turning up or down.
Consistency Consistency involves maintaining uniformity in design elements and interactions. Having the same graphical layout and button placement in all the screens of a mobile application, making it intuitive to navigate.
